The "Stay Safe Online" Survival Guide: A Student Digital Ambassador’s ChecklistAs students, we live our entire lives through our screens. From paying for a late-night snack at the mess to submitting our assignments on VITyarthi, our digital footprint is everywhere. But being "connected" shouldn't mean being "vulnerable." Here is a realistic, no-nonsense checklist to keep your data and your money exactly where they belong—with you.

1. The Golden Rule of UPI: PIN = PayingThe biggest mistake people make is thinking they need to enter a PIN to receive a payment. Let’s be clear: You never need to enter your UPI PIN, scan a QR code, or "authorize" a transaction if someone is sending money to you. If an app asks for your 4 or 6-digit code, money is about to leave your account, not enter it.

2. Treat Your OTP Like a SecretNo bank official, college administrator, or customer care executive will ever ask you for an OTP over a phone call or WhatsApp. If someone is asking for that code, they aren't trying to "verify" your account; they are trying to break into it.

3. The "Urgency" Red FlagScammers love to make you panic. If you get a message saying your "Bank account will be blocked in 10 minutes" or your "Electricity will be cut off tonight," take a deep breath. High-pressure tactics are designed to make you stop thinking clearly. Real organizations don’t operate through threatening SMS links.

4. Double-Check the "Verified" MerchantWhen you scan a QR code at a shop or for a peer-to-peer transfer, your UPI app usually shows a "Verified Merchant" name. Always look at that name before hitting 'Confirm'. If you’re paying a friend but the name shows a random string of numbers or a strange business name, hit cancel immediately.

5. 2FA is Your Digital BodyguardPasswords can be guessed or leaked, but Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) is much harder to bypass. Enable 2FA on your Gmail, GitHub, and LinkedIn accounts. It’s a 10-second setup that prevents 99% of unauthorized logins.

6. Avoid "Free" Public Wi-Fi for Sensitive TasksIt’s tempting to use open Wi-Fi at a cafe or station, but these networks are often unencrypted. Never log into your bank or student portal using public Wi-Fi. If you must use it, use a VPN to mask your data.

7. Clean Up Your Digital FootprintDon’t overshare. Posting your phone number, home address, or even your exact real-time location on Instagram or Snapchat gives attackers the "puzzle pieces" they need to impersonate you or target you with phishing.

8. If the Worst Happens: Report FastIf you lose money or your identity is stolen, don't stay silent out of embarrassment. Every minute counts. Immediately report the incident to the National Cyber Crime Portal at cybercrime.gov.in or call the 24/7 helpline at 1930.
